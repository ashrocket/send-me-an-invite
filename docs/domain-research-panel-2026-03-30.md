## 5. Analysis and Recommendation

### Winner: bookme.app (16 points, 0 negative flags)

**Why it wins:**
- Received votes from every skill level (tech 1/5 through 5/5) -- universally understood.
- Zero negative reactions from any panelist. No one was confused, put off, or embarrassed by it.
- 8 characters. Easy to type, easy to say aloud, easy to fit on a business card.
- Works across every use case: therapist, recruiter, freelancer, engineer, coach.
- Natural language -- "book me" is a phrase people already say.
- The `.app` TLD enforces HTTPS and is increasingly recognized by non-technical users.

**Risk:** `bookme.app` may already be registered (it is a very desirable domain). Availability must be verified.

### Runner-Up: bloomcal.com (10 points, tied with schedulewith.me and opencal.dev)

**Why bloomcal.com over the other 10-point domains:**
- `schedulewith.me` scored well but only with the professional/consultant personas. It skews formal and doesn't capture the playful spring theme that differentiates this product.
- `opencal.dev` dominated with engineers (3 first-place picks from 2 engineers) but actively alienated non-technical users (3 negative flags). It's the right name for the GitHub repo, not the product domain.
- `bloomcal.com` uniquely bridges the gap: it's professional enough for B2B consultants (Aisha voted for it), creative enough for coaches and designers (Sophie, Rafael), and the spring/growth metaphor is on-brand for the Easter theme without being aggressively seasonal. Only one person flagged it, and that was about it being "too themed for the OSS project" -- not for the product itself.

### Strategic Recommendation: Use Both Names

Consider a **dual-naming strategy:**

| Name | Use | Audience |
|------|-----|----------|
| **bookme.app** (or **bloomcal.com**) | Product domain, marketing site, user-facing brand | Everyone -- freelancers, consultants, non-technical users |
| **opencal.dev** | GitHub org, developer docs, contributor community | Developers, self-hosters, OSS contributors |

This is a common pattern in open-source products (e.g., Bitwarden the product vs. the GitHub org). The developer-facing name and the user-facing name serve different audiences and different stages of the funnel.

### The Pre-Selected Domains

Both `sendmeacalendarinvite.com` and `putameetingonmycalendar.com` scored **zero points** and received the highest number of negative reactions in the panel (6 and 5 respectively). The core issues:

- **Length.** At 26 and 28 characters respectively, they are extremely difficult to type, dictate, or fit on printed materials.
- **Spelling risk.** "Calendar" is frequently misspelled. Multi-word domains compound this.
- **Not shareable.** No panelist -- not even the most casual ones -- said they'd share these with a friend or colleague.

These domains work as joke/novelty redirects (funny to encounter once) but not as a primary brand identity for a product you want people to adopt. They could serve as fun easter eggs -- `sendmeacalendarinvite.com` redirecting to the real domain would be delightful -- but they shouldn't be the canonical address.

1. **Check availability** of `bookme.app`, `bloomcal.com`, and `opencal.dev`.

3. Register `opencal.dev` regardless for the open-source project identity.
4. Consider registering `sendmeacalendarinvite.com` as a novelty redirect -- it's a fun easter egg for the Easter-themed product.
